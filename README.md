## SYNERGY Case Study Competition – My Learning Experience

I recently participated in the SYNERGY Workshop Cum Competition, which involved around 300+ participants divided into multiple teams. This experience was not just about solving a case study, but about understanding how real-world problem solving actually works under pressure.

---

### Preliminary Round – Cinewave

Our first case was based on **Cinewave** and how customer behaviour can be used strategically.

We didn’t jump directly into solutions. Instead, we started by understanding the company properly and then approached the problem step by step using a data-thinking mindset:

* What is happening (Descriptive)
* Why it is happening (Diagnostic)
* What could happen next (Predictive)
* What should be done (Prescriptive)

We also used frameworks like **SWOT** and **PESTEL** to structure our thinking.

For me, the most interesting part was trying to find the **root cause** rather than just fixing what was visible — it honestly felt more like solving a mystery than just completing a task.

Team coordination wasn’t always smooth, so I had to take more responsibility to make sure things were completed on time. Finishing this round itself felt like a big achievement.

---

###  Final Round – Bharat Basket

We were selected into the **Top 10 teams**, which was unexpected and motivating at the same time.

The final case was on **Advanced Demand Forecasting and Inventory Optimization at Bharat Basket** — a topic that was completely new for us.

What made it challenging:

* Very limited time (less than a day)
* New and unfamiliar concepts
* High pressure to deliver

Even though it was difficult, we focused on understanding the problem quickly, tried different possible approaches, and made sure we completed all the requirements within the deadline.

---

###  What I Gained from This

This experience changed how I approach problems:

* I now focus more on **root causes instead of surface-level fixes**
* I learned how to **structure my thinking clearly**
* I became more comfortable working under **tight deadlines**
* I understood the importance of **taking ownership**, especially when things are uncertain

---

### My Final Thoughts

More than the result, this experience was about growth.

It showed me that real-world problems are not always clean or straightforward — but with the right approach, patience, and consistency, they can be handled effectively.

Definitely one of those experiences that helped me move a step closer to becoming better at **practical problem solving**.
